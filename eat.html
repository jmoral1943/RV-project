Icelandic Food

Iceland was founded by Iceland by Ingólfur Arnarson, and most of the settlers came from Norway. And since its difficult to grow alot of food locally they mainly rely on live stock such as lamb and seafood.

But Iceland still have so many lovely restuarants such as the Dill, VOX, and Grillid. Iceland even has some of the most delicous and different bread called 'Flatkaka' that is made of rye that is a thin round flatbred because it is difficult to grow grain in Iceland, therefore being Icelandic people they were resilient and found another way to make bread.

Some of the most famous food that Iceland makes best is there Skyr yogurt, the lamb, fermented shark, puffin, fish stew, and the coffee.

Dill

Awarding winning Restaurant

Dill is one of Iceland's most established restaurant. It has been awarded the coveted Michelin Star. Chef Gunnar Karl Gislason is the one responbile for attracting so much attention. Chef Gislason cooking of traditional Nordic ingredients with modern techinques makes it stand alone with a taste that is magnificent and foreign.

VOX

At VOX you will have a new exciting feeling just if you were to explore Iceland its some that is seems familar but different. VOX incorpates fusion of modern Icelandic and international classics, with the finest ingredients. Where the head Chef de Cuisine Snorri Victor Gylfason showcases classical, innovative, and colorful combinations. With a promise that they only use fresh ingredients. Grillid

Grillid is located within the Radison Blu Saga Hotel serves fine dining plates where you look out the window and peer into the beautiful landscape. Grillid is a grill restaurant on the eighth floor that you can either enjoy classic plates but with a new twist on them. surroundings from its eighth-story position. The menu here gives classic plates a contemporary update, with main courses such as fillet of lamb with pointed cabbage and crowberries, and white asparagus with tarragon and almonds. A four-course tasting menu comes with wine pairing that the restaurant has been lauded for.

Sjávargrillid

After Chef Gústav Axel Gunnlaugsson being named Chef of the Year in 2010 he opened his own restaurant in Reykjavik. Sjávargrillid translates to 'seafood grill' and Sjávargrillid mainly uses the food that was available to Iceland. They have multiple flavor combinations from mushroom and chili to grilled beef chuck and langoustine with potato.
